About

I am Sijia Cai, a PhD student in astronomy interested in galaxy evolution, star formation histories, and the baryon cycle in the nearby and distant universe. My current work focuses on connecting resolved galaxy structure with global evolutionary trends.

Research interests: galaxy evolution · stellar populations · infrared and optical surveys · statistical inference · observational cosmology.

Selected Publications

  1. Mapping Dust-Obscured Star Formation in Intermediate-Redshift Disk Galaxies

    Cai, S. et al. (2025), Astrophysical Journal

    We combine UV-to-infrared imaging to quantify radial star formation profiles and show how dust correction impacts inferred inside-out growth signatures.

    View on NASA/ADS

  2. Environmental Quenching Timescales from Group Galaxy Spectral Indices

    Cai, S.; Collaborators (2024), Monthly Notices of the RAS

    Using spectral diagnostics in nearby groups, we constrain delayed-then-rapid quenching channels and compare inferred timescales with simulations.

    View on NASA/ADS
